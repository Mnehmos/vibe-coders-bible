# Chapter 2 - Attention To Detail Was Always A Labor Constraint

Part: I - The New Discipline

## Thesis

The reason many projects lack tests, docs, diagrams, migration plans, and clean architecture is not that those things are unimportant. It is that detailed work consumes labor.

## Key line

AI does not remove the need for careful engineering. It makes careful engineering cheaper.

## Chapter job

Make the philosophical spine explicit. Detail has always been valuable, but detail competes with feature work, deadlines, fatigue, and scarce expertise. AI changes what can be afforded.

## Main beats

- Detail as hidden cost.
- Why rushed teams skip best practices.
- Why solo builders rarely have full documentation.
- Why refactoring, testing, and documentation lose to feature work.
- AI as a way to make previously expensive detail cheap.
- The new responsibility: if detail is cheaper, skipping it needs a better excuse.

## Labor-constrained detail table

| Practice | Why teams skip it | What AI changes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tests | Time-consuming to write | AI can draft broad coverage quickly |
| Docs | Always behind code | AI can update docs from diffs |
| Schemas | Tedious to maintain | AI can generate and cross-check contracts |
| Diagrams | Slow and manual | AI can produce Mermaid and architecture drafts |
| PR reviews | Cognitive bottleneck | AI can pre-review for obvious issues |
| Migration plans | Boring but critical | AI can write rollback and dry-run checklists |
| Threat models | Specialized labor | AI can draft first-pass risk maps |

## Draft notes
