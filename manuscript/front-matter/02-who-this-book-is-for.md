# Who This Book Is For

This book is for builders who want the speed of AI-assisted development without surrendering the engineering discipline that makes software survivable.

## New builders

You can now create software before you fully understand software engineering. That is real power. This book gives you a working map of the guardrails experienced teams use, without pretending you must become a senior engineer before you build anything.

## Experienced developers

You already know that tests, types, reviews, docs, and release discipline matter. This book explains why AI-assisted development changes the operating model: generation is cheaper, judgment is scarcer, and repo-level controls matter more.

## Team leads and founders

You need to manage agentic work without drowning in AI slop. This book gives you a vocabulary for delegation, validation, handoff, and control design that works across tools.

## Shared assumption

The reader does not need to worship AI or fear it. The reader needs to treat it as a labor multiplier whose output must pass through controlled commit boundaries.
